About

Scribblz! is a wall-traversing autonomous art-drawing robot with impeller suction and a full ROS2 stack for autonomous drawing and navigation. This was a 5-person team project where I led the software development and contributed to the mechanical design.

On the software side, I architected the system, designed the autonomous navigation, and programmed the robot. For the mechanical work, I focused on the physics and fluids analysis—running CFD simulations to redesign the impeller for optimal pressure force, researching and implementing different seal methods, and architecting the kiwi-drive system.

Personal Learnings

This project gave me substantial experience leading software design in a team setting and deepened my understanding of mechanical design. I learned a lot about fluids—particularly what matters for flow circulation, how to test effectively, and how to deliver a final product on schedule.

I also spent considerable time problem-solving sensors and instrumentation issues, trying out numerous localization methods until finding approaches that worked reliably for our use case.
