About

During my internship at Revimo, I focused on design for manufacturing where I adjusted mechanical components to optimize manufacturing time and reduce parts count. I machined numerous components on a CNC mill and created the CAM programs in Fusion 360.

I researched and evaluated new equipment such as wireless chargers to enhance the product's functionality. Additionally, I designed an automated testing setup to thoroughly test the device's performance and reliability.

One of the highlights of my internship was demonstrating the product at the Massachusetts State House, showcasing the innovative robotic mobility solution to government officials and stakeholders.

Testing Setup

I designed and built an automated testing setup to validate the device's performance and reliability. The system included custom fixtures and measurement equipment to ensure consistent testing protocols.

The testing setup allowed for comprehensive evaluation of the robotic mobility device under various conditions, ensuring it met all performance specifications before deployment.

Machining

I machined numerous components on a CNC mill, creating CAM programs in Fusion 360 to optimize toolpaths and machining efficiency. This hands-on experience with precision manufacturing was crucial for understanding the production process.

The machining work involved creating custom fixtures and jigs to ensure consistent part quality and reduce setup times between operations.

Demo

I had the opportunity to demonstrate the product at the Massachusetts State House, presenting the innovative robotic mobility solution to government officials and stakeholders.

This demonstration showcased the device's capabilities and potential impact on improving mobility for individuals with disabilities, highlighting the practical applications of the technology.
