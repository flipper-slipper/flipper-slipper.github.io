Mini Wind Tunnel

Spring 2025, Aerodynamics Project

Aerodynamics 3D Printing Data Acquisition

I designed and built a compact wind tunnel for aerodynamic testing and research. This project involved creating a functional testing environment to study airflow patterns and aerodynamic properties of various objects.

The wind tunnel features a carefully designed contraction section to ensure smooth, laminar airflow, along with a test section equipped with pressure sensors and flow visualization capabilities.

I used 3D printing to create custom components and integrated data acquisition systems to collect and analyze airflow measurements in real-time.

Design Process

I designed the wind tunnel with a focus on creating smooth, laminar airflow through careful consideration of the contraction ratio and test section geometry.

The design process involved computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations to optimize the flow characteristics before building the physical prototype.

Testing & Results

I conducted various tests to validate the wind tunnel's performance, including flow uniformity measurements and pressure distribution analysis.

The data acquisition system allows for real-time monitoring of airflow parameters and provides valuable insights into aerodynamic behavior.
