Innovative Hopper Design

For the "hopper project" at Olin College of Engineering, I designed a jumping device inspired by natural mechanisms. The project allowed for the application of rapid prototyping techniques, leading to continuous refinement of the design through various iterations.

My design was heavily inspired by the Springtrail hopper, incorporating an elastic rubber tail and a balloon-triggered mechanism for launching. The unique part of the design was in the control of the launching force, which allowed for a better performance with each iteration.

Over time, I refined the mechanism to increase the airtime of the hopper, making the device more consistent and predictable. The result was an elegant solution to a complex engineering challenge, where each iteration brought significant improvements.
